Shop storage cabinets are easy to underestimate because they are not glamorous. But as a learning project, they are excellent. They force you to work with repeated cuts, squareness, panel logic and assembly order — all while producing something genuinely useful.

## What this project really teaches

A cabinet is not just a box. It is an exercise in:

- repeatable cutting
- keeping references consistent
- maintaining squareness
- thinking about use before decoration
- assembling in a way that does not depend on last-minute correction

That makes it one of the smartest workshop projects a beginner can build.

## Keep the design disciplined

The easiest mistake is adding complexity too early:

- drawers before the case is under control
- decorative details before the structure is right
- internal divisions before the main dimensions are proven

A first good cabinet should be:

- square
- stable
- useful
- proportioned for what it will actually hold

Sophistication can come later. Utility should come first.

## My direct take

If you want a project that improves your shop and your method at the same time, build a storage cabinet. It may not be the most impressive thing on social media, but it teaches the kind of accuracy that carries into everything else.
