Table saws fascinate beginners because they symbolize a “serious” workshop. The problem is that they often enter the buying list too early. Before they become a good purchase, they can easily become a heavy, expensive and space-hungry one.

## When a table saw makes sense

A table saw starts making sense when:

- repeated cuts are common
- the workshop can actually host it
- a guided circular saw is becoming a real limitation
- you already understand support, setup and safety more than superficially

## When it is still premature

If your real situation is still:

- occasional sheet-good work
- small shop
- limited budget
- limited floor space

then a good guided circular saw is often still the more rational choice.
