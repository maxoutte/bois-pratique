A random orbit sander is one of the easiest tools to underestimate. Beginners often compare price first, then power, and only later discover that comfort, vibration control and dust extraction matter far more in real use. A poor sander does not just work slowly. It makes sanding feel worse than it needs to.

## What a good beginner sander should actually do

A sensible starter sander should:

- stay tolerable in the hand
- remove material without making the surface unpredictable
- work properly with dust extraction
- feel stable rather than vague

The goal is not aggression. It is control.
