Jigsaws are tempting early purchases because they look compact, flexible and easy to justify. The problem is that beginners often expect them to replace tools they do not replace very well. Chosen badly, a jigsaw offers more apparent versatility than real value.

## When a jigsaw actually makes sense

A jigsaw becomes a good purchase when you want:

- curved cuts
- occasional interior cuts
- a quick-to-grab machine for targeted tasks

If your real need is clean straight cutting on sheet goods, the priority usually sits elsewhere.
