People talk about the “right wood” as if there were a universally best choice waiting to be discovered. In a real shop, the right material is usually much less romantic than that. It is the one that matches the project, the environment, the expected wear and the amount of trouble you are willing to absorb.

## The first bad habit: choosing with your eyes

Beautiful grain can be persuasive. So can the appeal of “real wood” or the assumption that a more expensive material must be the serious choice.

But before buying anything, the useful questions are much simpler:

- will the piece live indoors or outdoors?
- is it visible furniture or purely functional shop work?
- do you need stability more than character?
- will the project be painted, screwed, joined or finished clear?
- does the budget need room for mistakes?

Until those questions are answered, “best wood” is mostly fantasy.

## Solid wood is not automatically the advanced choice

Solid wood has obvious strengths: beauty, texture, depth and presence. For the right project, those things matter.

But solid wood also demands more. Grain direction, movement, moisture and board quality all matter. In many early projects, that extra complexity is not helping — it is just adding another way to get in trouble.

## Sheet goods are often the more intelligent answer

In many shops, especially at the beginning, plywood and similar materials offer more practical value than prestige.

They usually bring:

- better stability
- more predictable flatness
- faster project planning
- fewer surprises than mediocre solid lumber

That may not sound romantic, but it is often what makes a project go smoothly.

## My direct take

For many beginner and shop-focused projects:

- choose plywood when you want versatility and fewer surprises
- choose solid wood when the visual and tactile result truly justifies the extra care
- choose MDF only when its strengths clearly match the job

The right material is not the most impressive one. It is the one that helps the project succeed without unnecessary drama.
